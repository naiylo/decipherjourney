Caesar Cipher

The Caesar Cipher is a simple encryption technique where each letter in the plaintext is shifted by a certain number. For example, with a shift of 3, A becomes D, B becomes E, X becomes A and Z becomes C.

A → D B → E C → F

Explanation

Notes Section: In the notes section, you can customize the letter mappings of the Caesar cipher. You can just write down a letter between A-Z in the box beside the letter and press the buttons.

"Try out your Combination": See what the text would look like with your mapping by replacing letters from the cipher text with your own ones. If you leave out a mapping it will be subsituted with a _.

"Clear": Reset your current mapping.

"Shift": Try out a shift value. Make sure to calculate backwards from the letter. For Example if the ciphered letter is F and you think it could be a A use a shift value of 5.

Cipher Text: Here, you will see the encoded text. Try to solve it by trying different shift values or mapping your guessed letters.

Deciphered Text: Once you input your cipher mapping or shift value, the "decoded" text will appear.
